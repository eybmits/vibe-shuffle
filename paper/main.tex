%%
%% The abstract is a short summary of the work to be presented in the
%% article.
\begin{abstract}
Music is widely used to regulate emotion, yet mainstream recommenders optimise
for long-run engagement rather than a listener's momentary affective state. We
present \emph{Vibe Shuffle}, a client-only web application that estimates a
listener's valence and arousal in real time---valence from facial expression and
arousal from optional heart-rate variability---and selects the next track to
match that state on Russell's valence--arousal circumplex. All signal processing
runs in the browser; no audio, video, or physiological data leaves the device,
and only anonymous self-report ratings are exported. We describe the affect
model, the track-ranking rule, and a blinded, counterbalanced within-subject
protocol that contrasts Vibe Shuffle against a Random Shuffle baseline drawn from
the same fixed pool, with perceived mood-fit as the primary outcome and liking as
a control. The system and its evaluation protocol are openly available, providing
a reproducible, privacy-preserving testbed for state-aware music selection.
\end{abstract}
